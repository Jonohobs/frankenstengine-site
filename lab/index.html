The Lab

Live experiments and proofs-of-concept on the way to a mixed-initiative 3D creation tool. What's here is rough, in motion, and built in public.

One engine, two modes, five use cases

The thesis: spatial selection as the bridge between manual and agent work in 3D. Paint a mask in 3D, tell the agent what to do, keep sculpting while it works. Nobody has shipped this paradigm cleanly. The factions on the homepage are the characters; the lab is where the engine that makes them gets built.

Mode 1

Create

Sculpt, kitbash, set-dress. Async-tolerant. Agent does heavy lifting in the background; the human does precision with a wand or controller. Head-tracked perspective sells depth without a headset.

Mode 2

Perform

Drive scenes live. Body trackers + voice puppeteer a digital character; the agent fills the gaps you aren't actively driving (idles, reactions, follow-through). Mixed-initiative puppetry.

Use cases (in attack order)

  • Standalone creation tool — solo creators, concept artists, indie devs.
  • Content creation — the tool is the content; sculpt + agent dialogue + performance, recorded.
  • Virtual D&D / VTT — mid-session asset generation with a solo-GM puppet layer.
  • Digital LARP — networked, multi-player, each viewer head-tracked. Heavy engineering, deep moat. (See: The Larper.)
  • Gameplay-feature licensing — embed in survival/crafting games. After the first two have proven the UX in public.

Experiments

Head-tracked Parallax

The screen as a window. Webcam tracks your face; the rendered scene shifts to maintain correct viewer-anchored perspective. Off-axis frustum + MediaPipe face landmarks. Runs entirely in your browser.

Digital Puppetry
